# Modifiers

**Moving the board around** (leans science):
- Convert tiles — turn a clump of one color into another, so you manufacture the matches you need.
- Destroy without matching — blow out a tile, a row, a column, or a chunk directly.
- Lock tiles — freeze a section so neither side can touch it for a turn or two.
- Shuffle or reseed — scramble the board, or salt it with the color you want and deny the enemy theirs.

**Bigger matches doing more** (any color):
- A match of four, five, or an L leaves behind a charged tile — clears a line, detonates an area, or counts as any color.
- Cascades build a multiplier that pumps whatever those matches were feeding.

**What charging does past full** (weapons, shields):
- Overflow — fill past the cap and the excess spills into something else (extra damage, a free secondary hit).
- Convert charge — trade built-up science into weapons, etc. Move energy between systems.
- Steal — drain the enemy's built-up charge instead of only denying them tiles.

**Effects that linger on the enemy** (weapons, science):
- Stacking damage that ticks every turn and ignores shields — pressure a turtle can't block.
- A mark that makes your next hits land harder, or one subsystem take extra.
- Weaken or jam — enemy hits softer, or their next skill just doesn't fire.

**Messing with the turn itself** (maneuver):
- Extra turns or chained moves off certain matches.
- An interrupt that cancels what the enemy has queued.
- Answering a telegraphed enemy move with the right match before it lands.

**A standing advantage you push** (maneuver):
- Positioning as a tracked state — matching maneuver tips it your way, being ahead grants passive edges (first strike, a flat dodge chance, more enemy misses), and the enemy's pushing it back the whole time.
